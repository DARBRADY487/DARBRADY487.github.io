Overview

The Loneliness Epidemic

We live in the loneliest age in human history.

According to numerous expert studies and surveys performed in recent years, the social disconnect in our culture is quickly becoming a modern-day epidemic.

Read More
Close

The General Social Survey by the National Science Foundation (NSF) found that 86% of millennials are feeling lonely and depressed, the number of Americans with no close friends has tripled since the 80’s, and almost a quarter of those surveyed stated that the number of people they can trust or confide in is “Zero”.

According to a Japanese Cabinet survey, there are currently 541,000 young Japanese aged between 15 and 39 who lead reclusive lives. These people are known as hikikomori -- a social term used to define those who haven't left their homes for at least six months.

Loneliness is an epidemic. Experts consider loneliness to be contagious, as people tend to become lonelier if they’re around other lonely people.

 

The ramifications are grave. The feeling of loneliness increases risk of death by 26%, according to the new study in the Journal Perspectives on Psychological Science. Loneliness and weak social connections are associated with a reduction in lifespan similar to that caused by smoking 15 cigarettes a day.

The Internet is considered to be the main culprit. In our digital age where everyone is connected to hundreds of “online friends”, but nobody is connecting in the real world -- people attempt to substitute real relationships with online relationships, but these connections tend to be superficial and ultimately dissatisfying.

Vision / Mission

Our Vision is to help cure this critical social problem, by connecting people together around all the fun social activities that are already part of our culture — recreational activities such as playing board games, sports or music together — and leveraging those real-world activities to connect people with other local, like-minded people who may become friends — people like their next door neighbors and work colleagues.

 

In order to break the modern-day epidemic of social disconnect, we chose the thriving Board Games vertical as our entry point.

Our Mission is to be the first real-world games platform of its kind, and the best online-to-offline service in the market. Our objective is to enable anyone to have a free and simple way to meet local people in a playful environment for a few hours.

Once we gain the required exposure in the Board Games community, we intend to expand our activities to other verticals.

 

So, whether it’s getting people together by playing board games in someone’s livingroom, by playing frisbee in the local park, or by playing some music together in the local community center — our Vision is clear and our Mission is set before us.

The Problem

For the millions of board-gamers in the world, there is still no easy way to discover, join or host a friendly local game for a few people.

Facebook events, Meetup, WhatsApp, Twitch and other web & mobile services currently been used to join and enjoy these game events, are too big and general to provide the results board-gamers are looking for.

 

The Solution

A dedicated platform where board-gamers across the globe can:

  • Connect with local people in their area for a playful get-together.
  • Discover and Join board games, card games, dice games, role-playing, etc.
  • Create and Host their own game event.
  • Share their game-events experiences with others.

 

The Technology

PlayPals is an Android and iOS app, based on React-Native and Firebase.

Read More
Close

The React-Native environment allowed us to save dev cost and time, to publish on Android and iOS simultaneously, while also presenting a great option to continue and build the next mobile versions, as well as the future web one.

We used the Firebase cloud services as it provided a simple and cost-effective solution for PlayPals’ current data, authentication, image hosting and in-app communications needs. Depending on our next stage of activity levels, users features, security needs and back-office requirements, we will re-evaluate our continued reliance on the Firebase service.

Revenue Model

Freemium for Users

Free users will get the core functionality, while Premium members will enjoy special features according to their personal or business needs.

The Premium Features could be: group creation & management, person-to-person exchanges, priority status for their game-events on Map and List Pages, expanded profiles and interactive lists, special local roles like Neighborhood Captain and City Chief, etc.

Local Game Stores / Bars / Cafés

Brick & mortar locations that serve their local board game market, will be able to promote their locations and services with a special icon on their city’s game map, by getting a dedicated page for their business, and by using native ads in the Game List and Event Pages.

These local businesses will also be able to get real-time maps and metrics on the game activity around their locations, to sell directly or offer discounts to local players, and to sponsor local game-events.

Global Board Game Publishers (Hasbro, Asmodee, Bandai, etc.)

From conversations we had with individual game publishers and CEO’s of global game companies, we learned that we all share the same goal – getting more people to play board games. But we also learned that right now, these game publishers don’t have any real-time funnel to games activity and to the people playing their games.

By using PlayPals data, these publishers would now be able to benefit from real-time metrics and maps on their games activity wherever and whenever they are played. The ability to Increase awareness to their brand or individual games, to communicate directly with those who played their games, and the option to create special testing sessions for their current and upcoming games.

These companies would also be able to benefit from direct e-commerce options, lead generation opportunities, the ability to sponsor game-events, or to use native in-app ad flow for promotion.

Food & Beverage Companies (and other advertisers)

There are many types of businesses that can benefit from knowing the time, location and number of participants of game-events happening around their physical location. The ability to connect and advertise to all those people with in-app native ads, by sponsoring local game-events, or by leveraging all the data, lead generation and re-marketing options.

For example:
Domino’s Pizza - can provide in-app coupons to all the PlayPals game-events being played in a specific radius from their stores. This will not only help with their bottom line, but will also improve their brand perception as one that brings people together around a social activity.

Starbucks - can provide free space for game playing and offer food & drinks discounts in times and locations where they experience activity decline. This will help them to fill out those locations, and improve their brand as the social third-place they are still trying to become.

The Marketing Plan

Our marketing plan, is based on first launching in a few North-American cities, and achieving a successful critical mass of users with all their local and social-media activities.
We should than be able to take all the activity we got as social proof, and use it to expand and launch in larger areas across the US and Canada, as well as to start the European cities launches.

We will first launch in cities that we choose to target according to board-game community activity, board game sales, social-disconnection levels, etc.
Cities like Seattle, San-Francisco, New-York, Los-Angeles and Miami in the USA. And Vancouver, Calgary and Montreal in Canada.

Pre-launch offline activities planned for target cities:

  • Meeting with game store/bar/café owners.
  • Participating in local game events and meetups.

On-going Online activities planned for target cities:

  • Advertising on a variety of social networks and channels.
  • Communicating with players and game groups whatever app, forum or platform they are using, and converting them from those to ours.

Note: From the 3 weeks test we did in Vancouver, we learned that we can reach a $1.8 CPA for a free user.
